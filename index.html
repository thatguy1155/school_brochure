MISSION

SPPS stands on a mission of independant learning. By providing a rigorous curriculum in a caring environment touting small student-to-faculty ratio, students grow up to be independant thinkers. It is our foremost goal to prepare students in every aspect so that they can develop the confidence necessary for success in their next stage of learning.

  • SPPS teachers are experts with hearts. Teachers are passionate about getting to know each and every student. Strong bonds between teachers and students build a vibrant community life and lead to exceptional learning in our classrooms and beyond.
  • All students, regardless of their interests or furure pursuits, are learning to develop their written and oral communication abilities for future success as scholars. SPPS incorporates various teaching methods to further develop communication skills so that students are able to think critically and express themselves logically as mature adults.

Academic Support

All students benefit from academic resources that aim to further develop their essential academic skills.These programs invariably improve students' confidence when approaching writing assignments, revamping math skills and/or receiving extra help on particular subjects. With guidance from academic advisors, students fully utilize such resources to best address their academic goals and aspirations.

  1. Writing Center

    Students can work with an English teacher or student volunteer in this 1:1 peer tutoring environment to receive assistance in all stages of the writing process, regardless of subject matter. Ultimately the writing center helps students become more effective and critical writers

  2. Math Lab

    In order to create a conductive ans supportive learning environment in our challenging math curriculum, the Math Lab is available any time during the school day.
    A student can refine his/her math skills and thus extend any or all concepts learned in class through a 1:1 tutoring session.

  3. Peer Tutoring

    SPPS peer tutoring is coordinated and run by student members of the national honors society with the purpose of helping peers through various academic needs. Through a systematic matching system, a student can receive one-on-one assistance on a particular subject in a small group setting, which promotes academic understanding and social development.
